# Bike Super Racing — `map_01` Excite Bike Polish Pass Notes

## Purpose
This note records the goals of the focused polish pass for the Excite Bike-inspired version of `map_01` core tiles.

The polish pass stays narrow.
It improves only:
- surface rhythm clarity;
- crest readability;
- ramp readability;
- internal dirt-noise discipline.

It does not expand the tile set.

## Why this pass exists
After the Excite Bike reference pass, the track direction became much stronger, but the following areas still had room for improvement:
- slope rhythm could be cleaner;
- crest shape could feel more confident;
- ramp silhouette could be slightly more obvious before contact;
- internal dirt pattern could be a little more disciplined.

## Goals of the polish pass
The polish pass must improve:
- top surface lip readability;
- rhythm of up/down terrain transitions;
- crest readability at gameplay scale;
- ramp readability as a clear takeoff element;
- overall arcade track confidence.

The polish pass must preserve:
- Excite Bike-inspired simplicity;
- strong sky / grass / track separation;
- low decorative noise;
- compatibility with `bike_01` readability.

## What must not happen
The polish pass must not introduce:
- more texture for the sake of texture;
- extra decorative tiles;
- complex terrain language that weakens instant readability;
- visual heaviness that competes with the bike.

## Review checklist
After this pass, review:
1. is the rideable surface easier to read instantly;
2. do slope changes feel cleaner;
3. is the crest easier to parse as a top point;
4. is the ramp more readable before contact;
5. does the bike still remain the most important gameplay object.

## Intended decision after this pass
If the answers above are strong, the current `map_01` core-tile set should be accepted as the Stage 2 baseline and the team should move to the finish set and background support.
