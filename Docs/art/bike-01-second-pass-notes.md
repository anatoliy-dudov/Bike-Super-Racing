# Bike Super Racing — `bike_01` Second Pass Notes

## Purpose
This note records the target of the second visual pass for `bike_01` after the first silhouette draft.

The second pass does not change the MVP scope.
It improves the same Stage 1 player vehicle with a stronger production-ready silhouette.

## Why a second pass is needed
The first pass proved the main direction, but still showed the following weaknesses:
- the front section was readable but too rough;
- the rider silhouette could be more unified with the body mass;
- the body shape was still slightly blocky;
- the overall bike profile needed a cleaner sport-bike feel.

## Goals of the second pass
The second pass must improve:
- front fork readability;
- front body shape;
- rider-to-body integration;
- sport-bike profile coherence;
- silhouette confidence at gameplay scale.

The second pass must preserve:
- instant readability;
- red body emphasis;
- wheel visibility on dirt background;
- compact arcade side-view shape.

## What must not happen
The second pass must not drift into:
- high-detail mechanical rendering;
- long thin realistic proportions;
- noisy contour work;
- decorative polish with no gameplay value.

## Review checklist after second pass
Check the following:
1. does the bike still read instantly as the player object;
2. is the front part cleaner and more intentional;
3. does the rider feel more integrated into one strong vehicle silhouette;
4. does the body feel more sporty and less blocky;
5. does the silhouette still work at browser-scale readability.

## Next step after second pass
After the second pass, the team should:
- validate the updated `bike_01` at gameplay scale;
- decide whether Stage 1 is ready for integration baseline;
- move to the next art batch only if readability is strong enough.
