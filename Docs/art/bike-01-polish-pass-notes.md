# Bike Super Racing — `bike_01` Polish Pass Notes

## Purpose
This note records the target of the focused polish pass after the second pass of `bike_01`.

The polish pass is intentionally narrow.
It improves only:
- body silhouette confidence;
- rider silhouette integration.

It does not reopen the full design of `bike_01`.

## Why this pass exists
After the second pass, the bike was already usable as a Stage 1 baseline, but two areas still had room for improvement:
- the body could feel more intentional and sporty in the rear and front transitions;
- the rider silhouette could merge more cleanly into one strong player-object mass.

## Goals of the polish pass
The polish pass must improve:
- tail cleanliness;
- body-to-front transition;
- rider torso cohesion;
- helmet-to-body readability;
- overall sport-bike silhouette confidence.

The polish pass must preserve:
- compact side-view arcade profile;
- red body emphasis;
- clean wheel readability;
- browser-scale clarity.

## What must not happen
The polish pass must not introduce:
- higher-detail mechanical clutter;
- heavy shading noise;
- realistic proportions that weaken readability;
- cosmetic complexity with no gameplay value.

## Review checklist
After this pass, review:
1. does the body feel cleaner and more confident;
2. does the rider read as part of one fast player vehicle shape;
3. is the silhouette stronger without becoming heavier;
4. does the bike still read instantly at gameplay scale.

## Intended decision after this pass
If the answers above are strong, `bike_01` should be accepted as the Stage 1 integration baseline and the team should move to `map_01` core tiles.
